# English Corrections — 2026-03-18

---

#### "이거 그러면 내가 제품만들어도 원가가 더 나가는거 아니냐"
- Better English: "Won't the cost of running the product exceed what we can charge?"
- Why: "원가" = "unit cost" or "cost of goods". Use "exceed" for comparison.

#### "우회할 방법이 없나 아예"
- Better English: "Is there absolutely no workaround?"
- Why: "workaround" is the standard term for bypassing a limitation.

#### "이거 전부 커밋 푸시까지 해다오"
- Better English: "Commit and push everything for me."
- Why: Direct imperative is natural in dev context.

#### "지금 올린거 보이냐"
- Better English: "Can you see what I just uploaded?"
- Why: "uploaded" is more precise than the literal "올린거".

#### "장난치는거냐 대화한거 기억은 하냐"
- Better English: "Are you kidding me? Do you even remember our conversation?"
- Why: "Are you kidding me?" expresses frustration naturally.

#### "세션 실행하면 뭘 계속 RUNNING하고 있는거냐"
- Better English: "Why does the session keep showing 'running'? What is it doing internally?"
- Why: Ask "what is it doing" to demand a concrete explanation.

#### "터미널 왜 안보이냐고"
- Better English: "Why isn't the terminal showing up?"
- Why: "show up" = appear/render on screen.

#### "엔터도 안눌러지고 똑바로 못하냐 진짜"
- Better English: "Enter key doesn't even work. Can you do this properly?"
- Why: "properly" is cleaner than "correctly" for expressing frustration.

#### "왜 아직도 Terminal에서 claude라고 타이핑이 안되냐"
- Better English: "Why can't I still type 'claude' in the Terminal?"
- Why: "can't I type" is the natural question form.

#### "일반 iterm2랑 완전히 동일하게 해야한다고"
- Better English: "It needs to be completely identical to a native terminal like iTerm2."
- Why: "identical to" + "native terminal" — precise technical comparison.

#### "왜 각 세션을 들어가도 동일한 화면이 나오냐"
- Better English: "Why does every session show the same terminal screen?"
- Why: "every X shows the same Y" — clear pattern description.

#### "미친거냐 하드코딩 절대 넣지마라"
- Better English: "Are you serious? Never use hardcoded values."
- Why: "hardcoded values" is the standard technical term. "Never" for absolute prohibition.

#### "왜 히스토리가 끊겼던거야 이런걸 어떻게 엔지니어링으로 개선한거야"
- Better English: "Why did the history get lost? How did you solve this from an engineering perspective?"
- Why: "from an engineering perspective" captures "엔지니어링으로" precisely.

#### "다시 들어가면 이전 세션이 종료되있잖냐 claude code 세션"
- Better English: "When I go back in, the previous Claude Code session is already terminated."
- Why: "terminated" is more technical than "ended" for process/session context.

#### "도대체 왜 똑바로 못한거니"
- Better English: "Why couldn't you get this right from the start?"
- Why: "get this right" captures the frustration of repeated failures.

#### "매번 logs 파일에 항상 우리가 배운 모든걸 기록하라고 이게 나의 자산이라고"
- Better English: "Record everything we learn in the log files every time. This is my personal asset."
- Why: "personal asset" captures "나의 자산" — knowledge as property.

#### "나는 아무것도 모르는 병신인채로 만들고싶지 않다고"
- Better English: "I don't want to stay ignorant. I want to understand what I'm building."
- Why: Reframe positively — focus on the goal (understanding), not self-deprecation.

#### "이거는 제1 지침이야 어떤 상황에서도 꼭 수행되야해"
- Better English: "This is directive number one. It must be followed in every situation, no exceptions."
- Why: "directive" is stronger than "rule". "No exceptions" = "어떤 상황에서도".
